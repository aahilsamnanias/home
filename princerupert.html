Prince Rupert

Location

The city of Prince Rupert is located in British Columbia on Kaien Island. It is near the center of the Pacific Maritime Ecozone.





























Climate

Identical to the rest of the ecozone, Prince Rupert experiences a Maritime climate since it is so closely situated to the phenomenal Pacifc Ocean. Consequently, it receives temperate temperatures and heavy, yet refreshing average of 2551.5 mm of rainfall during the year.



Spring

During the spring take a stroll in a brisk average temperature of 5.8℃.

Summer

In the summer, hit the beach in a warm average temperature of 12.3℃.

Autumn

During autumn, go fishing in a cool average temperature of 7.7℃.

Winter

In the winter, drink some hot chocolate indoors in a somewhat chilly average temperature of 1.6℃ with lots of rain.

















Landform

Prince Rupert is located in the Western Cordillera landform region as well as in the Pacific Ring of Fire.

The Coast Mountain Range passes through Prince Rupert. It provides beautiful scenery surrounding Prince Rupert. (Click on the image to the left to learn more about the Coast Mountains)

























Wildlife

The wildlife of Prince Rupert encompasses a large variety of distinctive species.

Mammals

Mammals are quite the common sight in Prince Rupert. Throughout the city, many species of deers especially the black-tailed deer can be seen roaming freely. Smaller species such as marmots can be seen almost everywhere, while larger species such as moose, black bears, and wolves are a rare sighting.

Birds

Similar to Vancouver, birds are extensive and important to the ecosystem. Many bird species such as ducks, owls and herons inhabit the city. In addition, bald eagles and ravens can be seen often within the city.

















Marine Life

Harbour Seals can be seen in shallow waters along the coasts and further from the shore there are several species of whales as well as other marine life living in the Pacific Ocean.

Soil

Like the rest of the Ecozone, Prince Rupert has leached soils, but the problem is especially larger in Prince Rupert as it is one of the wettest cities in Canada.

Human Activity

Prince Rupert a small city with a population 12,220 (as of 2011), is a port city which is very well-known for its transportation by ships.

Transportation

The transportation industry in Prince Rupert is its largest industry, partly due to the fact that it is so closely located to the Pacific Ocean. In addition, its port experiences minimal traffic congestion allowing ships to travel to and from the port with ease.























Fishing

The fishing industry, secondary to the transportation industry is one very large due to the neigbouring Pacific Ocean containing a diverse variety of marine life.

Tourism

It attracts tourists year-round with its admirable, warm climate and extraordinary scenery. One can visit the Museum of Northern British Columbia or go for a hike at Prudhomme Provincial Park.
