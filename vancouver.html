Vancouver

Location

The city of Vancouver is located in British Columbia, Canada at the southern tip of the Pacific Maritime Ecozone.





























Climate

Similar to the rest of the Pacific Maritime Ecozone, Vancouver experiences a maritime climate since it is so closely located to the Pacific Ocean. As a result, it experiences mild and welcoming temperatures throughout the year as well as quite a lot of precipitation annually (on average 1167.3 mm annually).



Spring

During the spring watch elegant flowers blossoming in a brisk average temperature of 9.1℃.

Summer

In the summer, kick back and relax in a warm average temperature of 16.6℃.

Autumn

During autumn, go for a hike in a cool average temperature of 10.1℃.

Winter

In the winter, drink some hot cocoa in a somewhat chilly, but refreshing average temperature of 3.7℃. with loads of rainfall.

















Landform

Vancouver is located in the rugged Western Cordillera landform region.



Vancouver is situated in the Pacific Ring of Fire like the rest of the Pacific Maritime ecozone. The Pacific Ring of Fire a semi-circular area surrounding the pacific ocean that is prone to earthquakes and volcanoes. Don't fear the majority of the volcanoes in the Pacific Maritime ecozone are dormant and there are no volcanoes in Vancouver. Despite this, there has been a history of several earthquakes in the Pacific Maritime Ecozone as well as in Vancouver.

























Wildlife

Vancouver contains a limited variety of wildlife since it such an urbanized city.

Mammals

The outskirts of suburban areas in Vancouver are home to the mighty Grizzly and Black bears which are wonderful to watch and occasionally, you may also encounter a wild coyote or wolf. For the most part, you will come across the typical urban animal such as a chipmunk or raccoon.

Birds

In Vancouver, birds are an integral part of their city as they are an indicator of a healthy ecosystem. Throughout the city, elegant geese and swans tend to roam around and you may frequently encounter a bald eagle, duck, or heron.

















Marine Life

Vancouver's marine life consists of mostly the wildlife of the Pacific Ocean since it is so closely located to it, but is home to other beautiful species of fish such as the Coho salmon, Red Snapper, and the Rockfish.

Soil

Like the rest of the Pacific Maritime ecozone, the soil in Vancouver is dominated with leached soil. Unlike other areas in the ecozone, leaching isn't a large problem in Vancouver as although it does receive considerably large amounts of rainfall, in other areas of the ecozone rainfall can be double of that of Vancouver.































Human Activity

Vancouver, one of the largest cities in Canada with a population of 631,486 (as of 2016). It's beautiful landscape and strong economy is only one of the many reasons why so many people settle in Vancouver.

Transportation

The transportation industry is one of Vancouver's largest industries, it generates over 172 billion $ annually and provides jobs for thousands across the city.























Film and TV Production

Vancouver nicknamed "Hollywood North" is home to North America's 3rd largest film and production center. Vancouver generated over 1.1 billion in revenue in 2011 and 260 films were produced in 2008 alone.

Tourism

Vancouver makes a marvelous tourist destination with mild climate and breath-taking scenery. In 2017 alone over 10.3 million people visited Vancouver. In Vancouver, one can do a variety of outdoor activities such as camping and fishing or visit Canada's only Olympic Museum.
