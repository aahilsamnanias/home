Overview

Location





The pacific maritime ecozone is located along the coast of British Columbia and on B.C's maritime islands as well as along its border with Alaska in the south-east area of Canada.

























Climate

Our mild temperature range attracts tourists and people worldwide, one of the warmest in Canada. During the summer experience warm, but mild temperatures. During the winter, no snowfall just enjoy cool, refreshing temperatures and breath-taking views of plentiful of rain. The neighboring Pacific Ocean is the reason for our mild temperatures, as a result we have a maritime climate.

Landform

The Pacific Maritime Ecozone is filled with a wide variety of terrain features such as valleys and plateaus, but is mainly composed of relief and rugged terrain partly due to the collision of the North American and Pacific Plate which created several extraordinary folds and mountain ranges.

The Coast Mountains, the most prominent mountain range in the Pacific Maritime ecozone extends 1600km and has been around for only 200-250 million years. It's highest point, Mount Waddington, is truly a spectacular sight.

The Western Cordillera landform region is one consisting of several mountain ranges, plateaus, and valleys. This extraordinary landform region covers part of the Pacific Maritime Ecozone and extends through Canada, the U.S, Mexico, and Central America. It's North American portion is called the North American Cordillera.













































Wildlife

The Pacific Maritime Ecozone is a relatively small ecozone, despite this it contains a diverse collection of wildlife with over 200 unique species.

Mammals and Birds

Mammals in the ecozone include elegant black-tailed deers, mighty black and grizzly bears, mountain lions, and wolves. Not to mention, this ecozone is home several species of birds such as the long-eared owl and red-tailed hawk as well as seabirds such as the glaucous-winged gull and pelagic cormorant.

Marine Life

The stunning Pacific Ocean, neighbouring the Pacific Maritime Ecozone is home to several species of marine life. Common species include: killer and humpback whales, sea turtles, fish (pacific salmon and pacific herring), and sharks. The native and very well-known dugong and the pacfic white-sided Dolphin live in the waters of the Pacific Ocean as well.

















Amphibians and Reptiles

The moist and warm conditions allow repitles and amphibians to thrive. Many species of frogs, toads, salamanders, and snakes inhabit this ecozone.

Vegetation

Due to the ecozone's heavy rainfall and warm and moist conditions throughout the year, several areas in the ecozone are an ideal environment for plants to grow.

In low-lying areas, the majority of forests are Western Hemlock Forests, which are forests dominated by the vibrant Western Hemlock tree.























In areas of high elevation (typically mountains), the majority of forests are Mountain Hemlock Forests, which are forests dominated by the stunning Mountain Hemlock tree. Due to the high elevation, there aren't very many Mountain Hemlock forests as the Western Hemlock forests.























Douglas Fir forests are small and quite dry and are located on the leeward side of mountains. These forests consist of mainly Douglas Fir trees. Despite their small size and dryness, these forests offer beautiful scenery due the symmetrical shape of each individual Douglas Fir.

Soil

Similar to the vegetation, the type of soil in an area depends on its elevation as there are frequent changes in elevation.

In lower altitude regions, the vast majority of soil is Podzolic soil which contains an abundance of humus. In addition, the soil is greatly leached due to the large amounts of precipitation, as a result there are several minerals and nutrients deep in the soil.

  

While soils in higher elevations aren’t very ideal as they lack nutrients once the altitude becomes around 900m. At this point, there is a lack of nutrients as many of these areas are cold, alpine areas receiving large amounts of snowfall.













































Human Activity

The stunning Pacific Maritime Ecozone, one of the smallest ecozones in Canada has a growing population of 3.7 million as of 2013. Many decide to settle here due to the desirable climate and several work opportunities in numerous industries.

Forestry

The forestry industry is the largest industry in the Pacific Maritime Ecozone. This industry involves lodging and paper production and over 330 communities in this ecozone rely on the jobs created by this industry.























Fishing

The fishing industry, secondary to the forestry industry is one very large due to the neigbouring Pacific Ocean containing a diverse variety of marine life.

Tourism

The spectacular scenery of landforms, mountain ranges, and ecosystems of the Pacific Maritime Ecozone make it an ideal tourist destination. Experience stunning sites such as the Coast Mountains, North Shore Mountains and the Kluane National Park of Canada as well as participate in several activities such as hiking, bird-watching, and zip-lining.
